The wonders1. These are either objects or marvellous helpers who can aid in getting them. In most cases it is clear that the owners won't give their possessions up, and can't be forced to do so, or won't participate. Still, Culhwch is cockiness personified2, so he gives it a go. The wonders sucessfully gained or performed in the tale are in bold below3.

  • 1) Harvestable food produced in one day from a cleared forest
    • 2) Amathaon the ploughman
    • 3) Govannon to deliver the irons
    • 4) Gwlwlwyd Chestnut Hair's Oxen
    • 5) Of these the reluctant Yellow Pale-White and Spotted Oxen
    • 6) Nynnyaw and Peibyaw, who were turned to Oxen

  • 9) The cup of Llwyr which can hold the world's strongest drink

  • 11) The horn of Gwlgawd of Gododdin to pour from
  • 12) The harp of Teirtu
  • 13) The Birds of Rhiannon, who wake the dead and lull the living
  • 14) The cauldron of Diwrnach the Irishman, to boil the meat4
  • 15) The tusk of the chief boar Ysgithyrwyn to shave with, taken while alive
