It should, however, be noted that there is a Ffynnon L(l)uan and ruined Chapel Llanlluan just outside Penrhiwgoch, north of Swansea**. While locals know this ruin as "Chapel of the Owls"*, apparently believing the name derives from tylluan ("owl": plural tylluanod), Thomas* suggests it probably reflects a, now forgotten, Saint Lluan.

Given these features, it is possible that the Llyn Liuan = Llyn Lliwan equality, or the derivation by Bromwich and Evans (see note 6), are incorrect. Either way, it is possible that the estuaries of Carmarthen Bay, which is near Llanlluan, might provide an alternative searching ground for this wonder. The lack of other chapels with this name might boost this suspicion by suggest a very localized cult for the hypothetical Saint Lluan, however Celtic saints aren't generally the stay-at-home type, and there's no other reason for supposing they should have just stuck round Carmarthen. Additionally, a Carmarthen location would have to argue against the strong suggestion that this was discussing the Bore.
