"The thirteenth is a quern which constantly grinds, except or Sunday, in Machlin in Cul. It is heard working under ground. The well of the grain is in Meadon, that is, a well from which grain flows without ceasing."1

The first part of this wonder was identified by W.J. Watson, a placename expert in the 1920s, as Acchenebron (now Auchenbrane) field (Achadh na brón 'field of the quern,') about three miles from Mauchline. The farm has now been split up, but a quick trip suggests the quern hasn't been heard recently, but the fact that it exists and doesn't grind on Sundays is still well known as local folklore. I had a good listen to the ground on the road near the farms, but sadly couldn't hear a thing...

It isn't clear, sadly, just whether the "well of the grain" is supposed to be in the same area, or a completely separate wonder; it seems likely it may be a separate wonder, actually associated with the next wonder in our list.
